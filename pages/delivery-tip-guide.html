Delivery Tip Guide 2026 — How Much to Tip Your Driver

Tip food delivery drivers 15-20% of the order total, with a minimum of $3-5 regardless of order size. Delivery drivers rely heavily on tips because base delivery pay is often very low — sometimes as little as $2-3 per delivery. Delivery tipping norms have become more standardized over the past few years. Whether you are ordering food, groceries, or a rideshare, drivers expect and depend on tips. Here is a breakdown by service type for 2026.

When Should You Tip More for Delivery?

Several situations call for a tip above the standard percentage. Drivers appreciate the extra consideration, and in many cases they are absorbing real costs and risks on your behalf:

  • Bad weather: Rain, snow, ice, extreme heat, or high winds make delivery dangerous and unpleasant. Add $3-5 on top of your usual tip, or tip 25% or more.
  • Long distance: If the restaurant or store is far from your location, the driver is spending more time and fuel. Tip at the higher end of the range.
  • Large or heavy orders: Multiple bags of groceries, cases of water, or large food orders for a party all mean more physical work for the driver. Tip 20% or more.
  • Late night or early morning: Drivers working outside normal hours often have fewer orders and face more inconvenience. A slightly higher tip is appropriate.
  • Apartment complexes or difficult access: If your building requires navigating gates, elevators, or long hallways, the delivery takes longer. Consider an extra $2-3.

How Does Tipping Work on Food Delivery Apps?

Most delivery apps like DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub prompt you to tip before the delivery. This pre-tip is visible to drivers and directly affects whether they accept your order. Orders with no tip or very low tips often get rejected by multiple drivers, resulting in longer delivery times and cold food.

You can adjust your tip after delivery on most platforms if the service was particularly good or bad. However, the pre-delivery tip is what the driver sees when deciding whether to accept your order. freetipcalculator.app helps you calculate the right amount before placing your order.

Should You Tip Rideshare Drivers?

Yes. While Uber and Lyft originally marketed themselves as tip-free alternatives to taxis, tipping has become expected and standard. Tip 15-20% for a normal ride. For long trips, rides to or from the airport with luggage, or rides in bad weather, tip at the higher end. A minimum of $2-3 is standard even for short rides.
